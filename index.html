Ernő Rubik

Ernő Rubik was born on 13 July, 1944 in Budapest, Hungary.

His childhood inspiration was his father, an internationally respected glider engineer.

He was a professor of architecture at the Budapest College of Applied Arts from 1971 to 1979.

He made the first Rubik's Cube prototype in 1974 as a puzzle for his students at the college.

The first prototype was made of 27 blocks of wood and rubber bands, and it took him a month to solve.

The toy was licensed to an American company in 1970 and became an international success during the 1980s.

It is one of the best selling toys with over 350 million sold to date.

Since his success, he has been involved in projects and foundations to promote STEM to students.

Besides puzzles, he enjoys sci-fi novels and growing succulents.
